A Brief History of Hipsters

***Warning: all information presented on this page may be, and probably is, factually incorrect***

Hipsters are a trendy group of young adults, typically associated with the millenial generation. They generally like old things for the sake of being quirky/different/whatever. For example, the beer Pabst Blue Ribbon, though it has declined in popularity since the early nineties and it is questionable whether the person who originally awarded it a blue ribon was qualified to do so, has seen a resurgence in sales due to hipsters having nostalgia for it, even though they were children or not yet born the last time PBR was popular.

Another element that defines the modern hipster is beards and moustaches. Generally speaking, hipsters will have very bushy beards and/or curly moustaches that hark to an era during which they, once again, were not alive and cannot actually be nostalgic for.

Hipsters are a continuously evolving group and their styles and affects may change with the times. Click here to keep up.

Interesting facts about hipsters:

  • They account for approximately 93% of domestic beard oil production annually
  • The average hipster consumes 3.6 gallons of Sriracha each year
  • They are responsible for the regentrification of parts of many U.S. cities
